Abstract

Advancements in 3D content generation have transformed tasks like video generation, multi-view synthesis, and 3D asset creation, but ensuring 3D spatial consistency remains challenging. Commonly used metrics, such as the CLIPScore, often fail to capture 3D consistency. To address this, we introduce CR-Gen, a novel framework for evaluating spatial consistency in 3D-generated content without requiring multi-view ground truth. Our approach involves pre-training on novel view synthesis tasks and fine-tuning to handle domain-specific challenges like dynamic and deformable objects. Additionally, we propose a keyframe-based user study method for video evaluation to ensure reliable assessments by minimizing content discrepancies between video pairs. Extensive experiments and user studies validate the effectiveness of CR-Gen across 3D generation, editing, and video scenarios.

Method

CR-Gen generates a per-pixel map to evaluate the generated image quality in multiple generative tasks, e.g. video generation, 3D multi-view generation and 3D editing, where ground-truth images are unavailable for evaluation.
